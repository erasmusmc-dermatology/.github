# Erasmus MC | Department of Dermatology

Welcome to the GitHub organization for the Department of Dermatology at Erasmus MC, Rotterdam, the Netherlands

## Research Mission
Our mission is to perform clinically relevant research to improve the diagnosis, treatment, and outcomes of patients with dermatological conditions. 

We utilize clinical, epidemiological, cost-effectiveness, qualitative, and translational research to:
* Detect conditions at early stages.
* Improve treatment and prognosis.
* Influence and change public health policies.

---

## Selected Work & Open Source Tools

### [Personalized Decision Making in cSCC](https://github.com/erasmusmc-dermatology/onco_ErasmusMC_model)
Development and validation of a clinico-pathological model to predict absolute metastatic risk in cutaneous squamous cell carcinoma.

### [D-ESMEL Study: Spatial Immune Microenvironment](https://github.com/erasmusmc-dermatology/onco_desmel_tme_code)
Codebase for analyzing the spatial immune microenvironment in early-stage melanoma.

### [Automated Detection of cSCC](https://github.com/erasmusmc-dermatology/onco_clam_addmil)
Weakly-supervised learning approaches for the automated detection of cutaneous squamous cell carcinoma in whole slide images of skin biopsies.

## Contributing
We welcome collaboration from the scientific and developer community. If you would like to contribute to our projects:
1. **Fork** the relevant repository.
2. Create a **Feature Branch**.
3. Submit a **Pull Request** for review.

Please ensure your contributions align with our goal of improving patient outcomes and maintaining high standards for clinical data integrity.

## Contact & Support
If you have questions regarding the models, datasets, or clinical applications found in our repositories, please reach out:

* **Technical Inquiries:** Open an issue in the specific repository.
* **Clinical & Research Collaborations:** [Contact us via Erasmus MC Dermatology - Departmental Website](https://www.erasmusmc.nl/en/research/departments/dermatology) or reach out to the lead researcher listed in the repository's documentation.
